Understanding Gynecomastia

Gynecomastia is the enlargement of male breast tissue, affecting men of all ages. Our specialized treatments are designed to restore a masculine chest contour with natural-looking results.

Advanced Techniques

State-of-the-art surgical techniques for precise fat removal with minimal tissue trauma. Ideal for patients with primarily fatty tissue.

  • Minimal scarring
  • Quick recovery time
  • Precise body contouring

Gland Excision

Surgical removal of excess glandular tissue through minimal incisions. Best for patients with firm, glandular gynecomastia.

  • Complete gland removal
  • Hidden incision placement
  • Permanent results

Combination Procedure

Combined liposuction and gland excision for comprehensive treatment. Recommended for most gynecomastia cases.

  • Comprehensive solution
  • Optimal chest contour
  • Natural appearance

Skin Tightening

Advanced techniques for excess skin removal and chest wall contouring. For severe cases with significant skin laxity.

  • Improved chest definition
  • Masculine contour
  • Long-lasting results

Gynecomastia Grades

Understanding the grade of your condition helps determine the most effective treatment approach.

I

Grade I

Small enlargement, no excess skin. Treated with liposuction alone.

II

Grade II

Moderate enlargement. Requires combination of liposuction and gland excision.

III

Grade III

Moderate to severe enlargement with excess skin. May require skin excision.

IV

Grade IV

Severe enlargement with significant excess skin. Requires comprehensive surgery.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is gynecomastia surgery painful?

The procedure is performed under anesthesia, so you won't feel pain during surgery. Post-operative discomfort is typically mild and well-managed with prescribed medication.

Will there be visible scars?

Dr. Jain uses advanced techniques to minimize scarring. Incisions are strategically placed in inconspicuous areas and typically fade significantly over time.

How long do results last?

Results are permanent when combined with a healthy lifestyle. The removed glandular tissue and fat cells do not grow back.

When can I return to work?

Most patients return to desk jobs within 3-5 days. Physical labor may require 2-3 weeks of recovery time.

What is the cost of gynecomastia surgery?

Cost varies based on the grade of gynecomastia and treatment required. Schedule a consultation for a personalized quote and treatment plan.
